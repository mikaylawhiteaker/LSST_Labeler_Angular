How to Classify

We are looking at asteroids from all over the sky. Sometimes the patch of sky will be crowded with background stars that might make it harder to identify whether the asteroid has a coma/tail as well as make it hard to identify which source in the image is the asteroid. We want to know about that. Look at the asteroid image (large image on left with purple crosshairs). If you see more than one source in the central region bordered by the crosshairs, answer 'yes'. About

LSST Labeler is a University of Portland Capstone project. The goal is to use human computing to label LSST images in such a way that is is easy for humans to help out.

To start helping us explore login above and start classifiying commets. At first we will check your work to help you learn what is and what isnt a commet.

Learn more about the telescope and the data you are seeing.

Check out our Github project to get a look under the hood.

This zooniverse project is an example of what we are trying to accomplish.

Thank you for participating!
